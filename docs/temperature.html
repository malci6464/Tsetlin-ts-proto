        Why Tsetlin handles this differently than deep learning

        Clause-based learning builds human-readable rules over binary sensor activations. Where a deep neural net would optimize thousands of floating-point weights, the Tsetlin machine toggles literal include/exclude states, producing sparse logic we can inspect.

        Transparent reasoning

        Each clause votes for or against “next hour warmer” using combinations such as warm seasonal surge AND afternoon sun gain while excluding cold gust anomalies.

        Data efficiency

        Binary clauses converge with far less data than recurrent networks because they exploit explicit domain priors encoded in the sensors.

        Edge-friendly footprint

        The trained model weighs only a few kilobytes, making hourly updates feasible on low-power weather stations.

        The comparison highlights how Tsetlin clauses trade a small hit in absolute accuracy for a dramatic reduction in parameters and energy draw—ideal for sensor deployments that require explainability and battery longevity.
